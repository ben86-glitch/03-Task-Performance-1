January:

January is the first month of the year and is named after the two-faced Roman god Janus.

March:

“March” is named for the Roman god of war, Mars. This was the time of year to resume military campaigns that had been interrupted by winter.

August:

August is the last month of summer in the northern half of the world. It is named after the first Roman emperor, Augustus Caesar.
